10 Statistical Methods

Each method answers a specific business question about your experiment.

1

Welch's T-Test

"Is variant B significantly different from A?"

Compares means without assuming equal variances. Uses Welch-Satterthwaite degrees of freedom approximation. Also includes z-test for proportions and chi-squared for contingency tables.

frequentist.py
2

Sequential Testing

"Can I peek at results without inflating false positives?"

O'Brien-Fleming and Pocock alpha-spending via the Lan-DeMets framework. Checking 5 times at alpha=0.05 inflates FPR to 14.6% -- sequential boundaries keep it at exactly 5%.

sequential.py
3

Confidence Sequences

"Can I monitor results continuously on a dashboard?"

Normal-mixture mSPRT confidence sequences valid at any sample size, including data-dependent stopping times. No pre-planning of analysis schedule required.

confidence_sequence.py
4

Bayesian A/B Testing

"What's the probability B is better, and what do I lose if wrong?"

Beta-Binomial for conversions, Normal-Normal for continuous metrics. Computes P(B>A), expected loss, and ROPE analysis via 100K Monte Carlo samples.

bayesian.py
5

Multi-Armed Bandits

"Can we auto-shift traffic to the winning variant?"

Thompson Sampling (optimal Bayesian exploration-exploitation), UCB1 (deterministic upper confidence bounds), Epsilon-Greedy (simple baseline). Reduces opportunity cost during experiments.

bandit.py
6

CUPED Variance Reduction

"Can I make the experiment faster by reducing noise?"

Regresses out pre-experiment user behavior: Y_adj = Y - θ(X - E[X]). Reduces variance by 1 - ρ² (typically 30-50%), equivalent to doubling sample size for free.

cuped.py
7

Multiple Testing Correction

"How do I avoid false discoveries when testing 20 metrics?"

Bonferroni (conservative FWER), Holm step-down (more powerful FWER), Benjamini-Hochberg (controls FDR, best for 10+ metrics). Without correction: 64% false positive risk.

multiple_testing.py
8

Power Analysis

"How many users do I need to detect a real effect?"

Sample size calculation, minimum detectable effect (via Brent's method), power curves, and duration estimation. Supports binomial and continuous metrics.

power_analysis.py
9

Novelty / Primacy Detection

"Is the effect real or are users just reacting to change?"

Weighted linear regression of daily treatment effects. Detects decaying effects (novelty) and growing effects (primacy). Prevents premature winner declaration.

novelty_detection.py
10

Segment Analysis

"Does the treatment work differently for different users?"

Per-segment A/B tests with Cochran's Q heterogeneity test using inverse-variance weighting. Detects when an overall null hides a strong subgroup effect.

segment_analysis.py

Key Design Decisions

Every choice has a rationale and a documented tradeoff.

✓

Welch's t-test over Student's t-test

Student's t-test assumes equal variances -- almost always wrong in A/B tests since the treatment changes the distribution. Welch's loses <0.1% power at typical sample sizes.

Correctness
#

SHA-256 deterministic hashing over random assignment

Same user always gets same variant with zero server-side state. Assignment can be computed at any layer (client, CDN edge, backend). Same approach as Google, Meta, Netflix.

Performance
λ

Both Bayesian and Frequentist as first-class citizens

Different teams have different statistical cultures. Rather than forcing a paradigm, the user chooses analysis_type at experiment creation. Mirrors how Statsig and Eppo work.

Flexibility
μ

Expected Loss + P(B>A) for Bayesian decisions

P(B>A) = 96% sounds great, but if B is only 0.001% better when it wins and 5% worse when it loses, the expected loss is high. Dual threshold: P(B>A) > 0.95 AND loss < 0.001.

Safety
↓

Pure-function stats engine with frozen dataclasses

No database imports, no HTTP dependencies. 303 tests with no fixtures or mocking. Reusable in Jupyter, CLI, or streaming pipelines. Immutable results prevent accidental mutation.

Correctness
↑

BigInteger PK on events table, not UUID

Events is the high-volume table. Sequential BigInteger inserts are B-tree friendly; random UUID inserts cause page splits and write amplification.
